# Spiral In-Store / Service Business Flow (V1)

## Overview

Spiral is a social-powered discount and attribution platform for brick-and-mortar and service-based businesses.

Merchants offer customers personalised discounts in exchange for verified social exposure (Instagram Story/post tagging the business).

Spiral does NOT process payments.

Customers continue paying through the merchant’s existing checkout/POS system (cash, card, Apple Pay, etc).

Spiral’s role is:

* customer verification
* discount calculation
* redemption tracking
* social verification
* analytics/attribution

---

# Merchant Onboarding Flow

Merchant signs up to Spiral and selects:

* Retail Store
* Service Business
* Restaurant/Café

Merchant then:

1. Connects Instagram Business Account
2. Configures discount settings:

   * follower tiers
   * max discount %
   * max £ discount cap
   * campaign settings
3. Adds services/products manually:

   * name
   * price
   * category
4. Spiral generates:

   * permanent merchant QR code
   * merchant code (manual fallback if customer camera broken)
   * printable Spiral branding assets/signage

IMPORTANT:
The QR code should remain permanent and should NOT regenerate when:

* prices change
* services change
* discount rules change

QR should simply point to the merchant’s live Spiral environment/merchant ID.

---

# Shopper Flow

## During Service / In Store

Customer scans merchant QR code or enters merchant code manually.

Spiral app opens merchant session.

Customer sees:

* merchant branding
* estimated discount available
* their follower tier
* current eligibility status

Example:
“You qualify for up to 15% off today at Joe’s Barbers.”

Spiral creates an active session tied to:

* shopper account
* merchant
* timestamp
* location/session ID

Customer does NOT need to post immediately.

---

# Merchant Redemption Flow

At checkout/payment:

Merchant opens Spiral merchant confirmation screen.

Merchant scans the shopper’s Spiral QR code.

Shopper profile/session appears instantly.

Merchant sees:

* shopper username
* profile image
* follower count
* discount %
* verification state

Merchant selects:

* products/services customer received

Example:

* Haircut
* Beard Trim

Spiral automatically calculates:

* subtotal
* discount amount
* final payable amount

Merchant presses:

# “Confirm Redemption”

This creates a confirmed redemption order/session.

Customer then pays normally using:

* cash
* card
* Apple Pay
* existing POS system

Spiral does NOT handle payments.

---

# Post Verification Flow

After redemption is confirmed:

Shopper account enters:

# “Pending Verification” state

Shopper must upload a verified Instagram Story/post tagging the merchant.

Verification requirements:

* must be public
* must contain valid merchant tag/mention
* must remain detectable by Spiral verification/scraper system

Close Friends Stories should fail verification because they are not publicly detectable.

Once verified:

* session marked complete
* shopper account returns to Active state

If shopper does NOT complete verification:

* shopper remains locked from future Spiral discounts
* shopper cannot start another redemption until previous one verified

Potential future additions:

* trust score
* cooldowns
* account quality scoring

# Merchant UX Principles

Merchant flow must be:

* extremely fast
* minimal taps
* no typing
* visually obvious

Merchant screen should be optimized for:

* tablets
* iPads
* counter displays

The experience should feel similar to:

* loyalty redemption
* student discount validation
* coupon confirmation

NOT payment processing.

---

# Key Product Decisions

## Spiral is NOT:

* a payment processor
* a POS replacement
* a fintech product

## Spiral IS:

* a verified social-to-sale attribution platform
* a social-powered discount layer
* a customer exposure/reach engine

---

# Important Operational Decisions

## QR Code

Permanent and stable.

Should not regenerate for:

* pricing changes
* campaign edits
* service updates

Only regenerate for:

* security reset
* fraud concerns
* manual merchant request
* multi-location/campaign variants

---

# Recommended Early Merchant Categories

Best launch categories:

* barbers
* salons
* tattoo studios
* gyms
* cafés
* aesthetics clinics
* fashion boutiques
* car detailing

These naturally generate social content.

Restaurants are possible but operationally more complex due to:

* split bills
* group tables
* shared meals
* multi-person attribution

---

# Suggested Merchant Signage

Spiral signage should be subtle and premium.

Examples:

* mirror decals
* menu icons
* countertop displays
* “Spiral Accepted Here”

Avoid overly influencer-style branding.

Goal:
Make Spiral feel like modern loyalty/rewards infrastructure rather than “post for discounts.”

# Core System Objects

## Merchant

* merchant_id
* business info
* Instagram account
* pricing/services
* discount rules

## Shopper

* shopper_id
* linked social accounts
* follower count
* verification status
* pending redemption status

## Redemption Session

* session_id
* merchant_id
* shopper_id
* selected products/services
* subtotal
* discount amount
* final payable amount
* redeemed timestamp
* verification status

## Social Verification

* story/post detected
* merchant tag validated
* visibility confirmed
* timestamp
* verification result

---

# Key UX Insight

Spiral sessions should ideally begin DURING the experience, not only at checkout.

Examples:

* barber mentions Spiral during haircut
* diner notices Spiral logo on menu before food arrives
* customer takes photo during service experience

This creates more natural social posting behaviour.
